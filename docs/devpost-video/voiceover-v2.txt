Travel planning rarely starts with a perfect itinerary. It starts with a feeling, a confirmation email, or a small question: what should I do next?

This is Voya, an agentic travel companion that turns scattered travel details into a verified, living journey.

On Inspiration, the traveler describes a mood instead of choosing a destination. Voya can turn something as simple as “ocean air and a feeling of discovery” into a focused set of possibilities.

When plans already exist, Smart Import accepts a file, pasted booking text, or a photo. The confirmation becomes structured trip data instead of another item buried in an inbox.

Voya then assembles one clear timeline. The traveler can see the destination, dates, bookings, current status, and the next useful action without switching between multiple apps.

The Assistant is powered by Trip Guardian. Bounded specialist agents watch the journey, review current context, and merge important findings into a short list of actions. The product clearly separates published schedule information from live provider data, so uncertainty remains visible rather than being hidden behind confident language.

I used Codex as the engineering partner throughout Build Week: exploring the codebase, implementing product flows, connecting services, debugging the iOS application, running tests, and preparing the submission assets.

GPT-5.6 powers the reasoning workflows behind Voya. It helps interpret unstructured travel context, coordinate specialist agents, and produce grounded, useful recommendations while preserving clear boundaries around what is known.

Voya is not another place to store a booking. It is the layer that keeps a journey understandable, current, and ready for the next best action.
